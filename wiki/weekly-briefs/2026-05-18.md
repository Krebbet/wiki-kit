# Trends in the industry

- **The surveillance-pricing legislative cluster gained a fourth doctrinal axis — and lost its private-enforcement teeth.** Colorado HB26-1210 (passed both chambers May 2026) is the first instrument to fuse *consumer pricing* and *algorithmic wage-setting* into a single prohibition on "price or wage setting algorithms," enforced as a deceptive trade practice under the Colorado Consumer Protection Act. The labour side now sits in the same statute as the pricing side — a scope expansion none of MD HB0895 / NY APDA / CT SB4 have. But the private right of action in the original draft was **struck by Senate amendment**: every enacted instrument in the cluster (MD/NY/CT/CO) is now AG-enforcement-only, which materially weakens the "class-action standing" framing the wiki had been tracking. Calmatters' May 15 synthesis frames the whole 2026 wave as affordability-politics unlocking what stalled entirely in 2025. Captured to [[counter-power/regulatory-responses]].

- **Algorithmic information-sharing antitrust enforcement jumped industries (housing → meat) — and the remedy itself is now the contested object.** The DOJ + six-state Agri Stats consent decree (proposed final judgment, D. Minn.) is structurally a RealPage twin: a hub-and-spoke benchmarking intermediary whose seller-only access enabled coordinated pricing without explicit cartel comms. New dedicated page [[counter-power/agristats-consent-decree]] enumerates the five-component remedy (sales-data prohibition; anonymisation floor ≥3 contributors / no single >70%; data-aging ≥45-day lag; open/non-discriminatory access at processor-equivalent price; 7-yr defendant-funded monitor). *(synthesis)* The load-bearing development isn't the decree — it's that the open-access remedy is itself attacked: Dayen / Lee Hepner (AELP) argue it *expands* Agri Stats' paying customer base while leaving the processor-ranking reports and consulting relationships intact ("laundering the cartel"), with ATPCO 1992 as the same failure mode. This forced the wiki's **first conflict file**, [[conflicts/information-sharing-remedy-efficacy]] — sharpening the wiki's stance from "DOJ enforcement = counter-power lever" to "remedy design is load-bearing and can invert."

- **ACA theory matured from empirical to formal — and the result is strategically directive.** Battiloro et al. 2026 (arXiv 2605.06749) delivers the first formal statistical lower bounds for *M* competing/collaborating collectives manipulating a shared classifier (generalising Gauthier et al. ICML 2025 from M=1). The actionable finding: **label coordination dominates raw mass** — fragmented collectives targeting different labels at shared feature values cancel toward near-zero global success. Direct strategy-layer input: the [[strategies/data-disruption-strategy-map]] / [[strategies/mechanism-synthesis-readout]] multi-collective builds must foreground label alignment as a prerequisite, not assume mass suffices. Extended into [[mechanisms/algorithmic-collective-action]] with a regime-dependence annotation on the prior Karan ≤75% figure.

- **Cooperative federation as the capital-conundrum answer is no longer a CoopCycle monoculture.** Drivers Cooperative–Colorado (~1,200 drivers, ~2K rides/mo, 80/20 split) has an executive-director-stated roadmap toward a *nationwide federation of driver co-ops sharing technology and financial infrastructure* — the rideshare analogue to CoopCycle's federation-of-locals model, now in active design rather than aspiration. Plus a replicable demand-side anchor: a pending public-agency (RTD) contract proposal. Extended into [[mechanisms/platform-cooperatives]] as the second live in-progress federation case.

- **DSAR enforcement is acquiring a sharper, more portable legal theory.** *(synthesis)* noyb's LinkedIn Art.15-paywall complaint (Austrian DPA, May 2026) pins access-rights denial to the platform's *own monetisation behaviour*: if a company tracks **and** sells the resulting data via a paywall, it cannot invoke data-protection grounds to refuse a free Art.15 request. That's a reusable model-complaint template portable to any platform paywalling personalised data — it hardens the wiki's Tier-1 DSAR-coordination build from aspiration toward a concrete artefact. Extended into [[organizations/noyb]].
