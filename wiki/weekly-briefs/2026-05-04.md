# Trends in the industry

- **Latent reasoning has fractured into three competing substrates this week.** [[latent-grpo]] (Tsinghua/Alibaba) lands continuous-token GRPO with three stabilization fixes; LEPO (ACL 2026) does continuous via Gumbel-Softmax stochasticity; "Thinking Without Words" uses *discrete* abstract tokens from reserved vocabulary as a compressed scratchpad. Three different mechanisms, all on arXiv within the same 7 days, all framed against standard CoT. The field has no consensus on what "efficient reasoning without words" means at the architecture level — and the three papers don't share training machinery. *(synthesis)*
- **The GRPO-line credit-assignment fix pattern is now structural, not incidental.** [[agentflow]]'s Flow-GRPO broadcasts trajectory-level outcome reward across multi-turn modules; [[neural-garbage-collection]] last week did the same trick for KV-cache eviction; [[token-gradient-cancellation]] for within-trajectory tokens. The recurring move — broadcast a sparse outcome reward to all the latent decisions that contributed — is now the way RL is unlocking sequential-decision problems that GRPO alone broke on. Latent-GRPO's manifold-aware advantage masking is a fourth instance of the same pattern in latent space. *(synthesis)*
- **Test-time training has split into "modify the architecture" vs "modify the training procedure" camps.** [[in-place-ttt]] (last month) and [[ssm-tool-use-length-generalization]] (this week) both keep the architecture roughly intact and *change what the model does at inference*. [[tempo-test-time-rl]] keeps the architecture intact and *changes how the actor-critic loop is anchored* (EM with periodic critic recalibration). The Apple SSM-tool-use paper takes the most radical position: don't change the architecture *or* the training procedure, just give the model a Turing-tape tool. Three live paths now coexist for "cure fixed-state limits at inference."
- **Conference-week signal is real and persistent.** Two of this week's five captures (AgentFlow 2510.05592, To Infinity and Beyond 2510.14826) are October 2025 arXiv submissions trending *now* because they're ICLR 2026 Orals (top 1.1%). Same pattern as last week's Mamba-3 + GEPA. ICLR 2026 finishes May 4 — expect the next two weeks to show preprint dates further back as the conference-vetted picks keep surfacing. Worth biasing the trend scan toward OpenReview / ICLR oral lists for one more sweep, then it'll quiet.
- **Static 3D priors from 2D generation are now empirically established.** [[vision-banana]] (GDM, Kaiming + Saining) shows zero-shot SOTA on metric depth, surface normals, and segmentation — δ1=0.929 on depth without camera intrinsics, beating Depth Anything V3 — by lightweight instruction-tuning of an image generator. This is the strongest direct evidence that 2D image-generation pretraining encodes 3D structure. Sibling papers Tuna-2 (encoder-free multimodal) and World-R1 (Flow-GRPO for video gen with 3D rewards) form a three-paper cluster on the "generation as perception" axis. The static-3D-priors result partially undermines [[moonlake-world-models]]'s position post; the harder *interactive* simulation claim remains open.
- **The fixed-state-SSM debate now has a formal proof on the Position-A side.** [[ssm-tool-use-length-generalization]] (Apple, ICLR Oral) **proves Theorem 2.1**: any fixed-memory recurrent architecture (Mamba/LSTM/RetNet/H3/local-attention) cannot solve long-form generation in CoT-only or single-turn-tool-use settings. This converts the Titans/Hope assertion into an impossibility result. The paper *also* introduces a third escape: don't fix the SSM, don't add attention, just give it a Turing-tape tool. Mamba-1.4B trained on ≤5-digit addition extrapolates to 1000-digit at 100% under interactive tool-use. *(synthesis)*
- **Reward modeling is becoming the new bottleneck framing for post-training.** ThinkPRM (generative verbalized step-PRM) and "Reward Models Are Secretly Value Functions" (temporal-coherence RMs) are both trending alongside [[tempo-test-time-rl]]'s critic-recalibration result — three concurrent fixes for the same diagnosed problem (sparse-or-drifting reward signal for long reasoning trajectories). A year ago the field's perceived bottleneck was "the RL algorithm." This week three papers say it's the reward signal.

The credit-assignment trick is the load-bearing contribution; modular decomposition is the rest. *(new, arXiv:2510.05592)*
