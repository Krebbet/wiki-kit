# Trends in the industry

- **Counter-algorithmic tooling cadence went quiet this week — but the cleanest single tool drop of the year landed.** No in-window tier-1 arXiv hits on cs.CY / cs.CR for adversarial / DSAR / fingerprint-defense / poisoning; no major tracked-tool releases beyond Nightshade 1.1's bug-fix. The exception is **Berkman Klein's Keyring** (April 16 launch; Harvard Gazette feature in window) — the first **production-grade consumer reference implementation** of the W3C DID/VC pattern documented at [[mechanisms/decentralized-agent-identity]]. ASML/Linux Foundation Decentralized Trust Graph co-build, on-device biometric + selective-disclosure proofs (age-band not birthdate; account-ownership not username), GitHub `berkmancenter/keyring-wallet`. ASML PI James Mickens + product team frame it explicitly: *technology is solved; institutional adoption is the bottleneck because incumbents benefit from owning your data.* That's the best-sourced public confirmation of the issuer-governance unsolved-problem this wiki has been flagging.

- **Regulation-side: disclosure-laws-plus-AG-enforcement is producing actual platform behavioural change, while prohibition-laws-have-loopholes is the counter.** NY's **Algorithmic Pricing Disclosure Act** (effective Nov 10 2025; mandated phrase verbatim *"THIS PRICE WAS SET BY AN ALGORITHM USING YOUR PERSONAL DATA"*; placement standard *"clearly and conspicuously near prices"*) was operationalised in January 2026 as a DOM-testable benchmark via NY AG Letitia James's demand letter to Instacart. Following the December 2025 Groundwork Collaborative + Consumer Reports field study (**437 shoppers / 4 cities / 73% of items at multiple prices / 23% max differential / ~$1,200/yr per family of four**), Instacart **ended all "item price tests"** — though partner-level promotion testing continues. The counter: Cory Doctorow's April 30 2026 Pluralistic post (synthesising Pat Garofalo / American Economic Liberties Project) catalogues **six structural loopholes in Maryland HB0895** (grocery-only scope; consent-via-clickwrap; undefined promotional/temporary-discount exemptions; loyalty-card exemption; subscription exemption; no private right of action + pre-emption of MD CPA). The "first-in-nation prohibition" framing the wiki captured 2026-04-23 is now **qualified, not overturned** — prohibition exists on paper; enforcement reach is materially narrower than political framing implied.

- **Strategy-layer: the Solidarity Stack lands as cross-layer narrative architecture for cooperative AI.** Trebor Scholz's keynote at **Cooperative AI Conference Istanbul, November 11 2025** (PCC's 11th annual convening; published as PCC blog Apr–May 2026) names a **seven-layer cooperatively-owned counter-architecture** spanning Earth (cooperative mines / renewable-energy commons in Colombia, Chile, South Africa, Austria) → Digital Infrastructure (Hostsharing eG, Ashton Data Center Cooperative) → Data (MyData / cooperative data trusts) → Algorithmic Development (Facttic, Outlandish, Patio) → Labour (African Tech Worker Cooperative post-Meta/Sama lawsuit) → Application (Sicredi Ouro Verde, Arla Foods) → Governance (modelled on **SWIFT** as the working planetary-scale cooperative-protocol precedent). Federation primitive: **Solidarity Stack Circles → 2026 global online course → democratic planetary organization.** The wiki's first cross-layer narrative architecture for cooperative AI infrastructure — gives [[mechanisms/agent-interop-protocols]] a planetary-scale cooperative-governance precedent it didn't previously have. *(synthesis)*

- **Bharat Taxi state change confirmed: gov-coop hybrid revenue-testing has begun.** Passenger booking went live in Delhi this window with 51,000+ drivers registered; Gujarat onboarding in progress; six-city initial scope (Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, Chennai, Hyderabad, Kolkata) with phased tier-2 + rural expansion planned. ZeeBiz second-source corroboration introduces the operating-entity name (**Sahakar Taxi Cooperative Limited**, MSCS Act registration June 6 2025) and the eight sponsoring cooperative-federation list (AMUL, IFFCO, KRIBHCO, NAFED, NDDB, NCEL, NCDC, NABARD). First operational-stage update on the gov-coop hybrid 5th type the wiki captured 2026-04-27. Open questions remain: cost structure, pricing-algorithm architecture (flat-rate vs surge), and whether take-rate is genuinely zero or recovered through other channels.

- **Empirical compliance / behavioural-evidence baseline expanded.** The Groundwork/CR Instacart study extends the cross-jurisdictional empirical pattern that has been forming on the wiki since April: webXray California GPC audit (86% Google / 69% Meta / 50% Microsoft non-compliance) + noyb 83.5% GDPR Article 15 access-request failure + now Groundwork/CR (Instacart 23% max same-product differential at same store same time, 73% of items at multiple prices, ~$1,200/yr per family of four).
