# Trends in the industry

- **RLVR is a clipping artefact as much as a learning signal.** Spurious Rewards (2506.10947) shows GRPO delivers 73% of full-reward gains with *random* rewards on Qwen, because the clip term asymmetrically amplifies high-prior pretraining behaviours — connecting directly to [[research/rlvr-mechanics/binary-rewards-rl-challenges]] (I-projection collapse) and [[research/rlvr-mechanics/rethinking-rl-sparse-selection]] (token-reranking). The implication for the thesis: reward design matters less than clipping mechanics, but only for Qwen-family models — zero transfer to Llama3/OLMo2, flagging Qwen-centric RLVR benchmarking as unreliable.

This is the mechanistic confirmation the thesis needed: the "single example imprints a concept" hypothesis is grounded in representational geometry, not just behavioural similarity.
