# Trends in the industry

- **RL post-training is being attacked from three angles at once this week — and one of them is "stop doing RL".** [[reasonmaxxer]] (USC + DEVCOM ARL) shows that RL touches only 1–4% of tokens, all within the base model's top-5, all at high-entropy decisions; an oracle experiment causally reproduces RL accuracy by replacing only those 1–4% positions. ReasonMaxxer's RL-free entropy-gated contrastive FT matches full RL across 3 model families × 6 scales × 6 math benchmarks at **$4–$25 vs $200–$103,000**. Sibling on the watchlist: IOP-GSPO derives process supervision from failed rollouts (outcome→process); UCPO adds a uniformity penalty over the *correct-solution distribution* and identifies mode collapse as a structural property of GRPO objectives, not a training artifact; ResRL uses SVD-projected negative-sample gradients. Four concurrent papers, four diagnoses, one consensus: standard GRPO/PPO applies gradient to the wrong places. *(synthesis)*

- **RL reasoning has a scaling law now.** [[scalelogic]] establishes a clean power-law `T ∝ D^γ` (R² > 0.99) with γ ranging from 1.04 (implication-only) to 2.60 (with quantification). Doubling reasoning depth multiplies training cost by 2× to 6× depending on logical expressiveness. The headline downstream result is qualitative: the most-expressive curriculum transfers **+10.66 pp** across 8 math/reasoning benchmarks; simpler curricula plateau at +2–3 pp regardless of compute. The field's framing shifts from "scale compute" to "curate task structure". The result holds across GRPO, DAPO, GSPO — algorithm-agnostic regularity.

- **Three independent escapes from the fixed-state long-context bottleneck landed in 7 days.** [[sst-v2]] (Fifth Dimension) keeps a Transformer backbone and adds per-layer FFN-driven nonlinear horizontal state — +15.15 pp GPQA-Diamond with a Gemma-3 27B QLoRA trained only on GSM8K (6,579 examples, single RTX PRO 6000). [[memagent]] (ByteDance Seed / Tsinghua, ICLR Oral) keeps the Transformer *and* its attention as-is and instead trains an agent to overwrite a 1024-token plain-text memory buffer via Multi-Conv DAPO; 8K training context extrapolates to 3.5M-token QA with <5% loss. Apple's [[ssm-tool-use-length-generalization]] proved (last week) the Theorem 2.1 impossibility result for GSSMs and offered a *tool-augmented* third path. Three load-bearing solutions to the same problem, each architecturally distinct — [[conflicts/fixed-state-ssm-long-context]] now has Positions A, B-hybrid, B-tool, B-RL-overwrite, C-nonlinear-horizontal. *(synthesis)*

- **The diffusion-LM line is no longer a curiosity.** [[coladlm]] (ByteDance Seed) and watchlist siblings Joint Latent DLM (2605.07933) and Break the Block (2605.02263) all decouple semantic planning (continuous latent) from token realization, and apply RL on top to fix dynamic block sizing. ColaDLM's best-scaling-curve result at ~2000 EFLOPs vs matched AR + LLaDA across 8 benchmarks is the first credible "continuous-latent diffusion catches AR at compute scale" datapoint (under few-shot generative eval — PPL gap vs AR remains structural and the authors flag it as such). [[tidar]]'s discrete-token hybrid is the immediate counterpart; eval protocols differ enough that direct contradiction is premature, but "which non-AR paradigm succeeds AR?" is now a live three-way race.

- **ICLR 2026 post-conference signal is producing recursive credit-assignment papers.** [[memagent]] is a July 2025 arXiv hit trending now because of its Oral status. Watchlist additions RAO (Recursive Agent Optimization, 2605.06639) and IOP-GSPO (2605.05226) both broadcast outcome reward through multi-conversation rollouts — the same trick [[agentflow]]'s Flow-GRPO uses to escape long-horizon variance. The pattern from last week ("broadcast a sparse outcome reward to all latent decisions that contributed") is now structural: Flow-GRPO at the multi-turn level, Multi-Conv DAPO at the multi-conversation level, and IOP-GSPO at the trajectory-repair level. *(synthesis)*

- **Mechanistic interp is starting to deliver actionable agent results.** Watchlist addition "Tool Calling is Linearly Readable and Steerable" (2605.07990) finds that *tool selection is linearly encoded in activations before generation* across 12 models (270M–27B), with 77–100% steering accuracy via mean-difference vectors. Even untrained base models internally encode the correct tool at 69–82% before their generation accuracy is 2–10%. The bottleneck for tool-using agents may not be representation — it's decoding. This complements the watchlist's reward-modeling cluster (ThinkPRM, Reward-Models-Are-Value-Functions) on "the bottleneck is signal extraction, not signal generation."

# Top 3 from the watchlist

1. **ReasonMaxxer** — USC + DEVCOM ARL. RL touches only 1–4% of tokens, all in base top-5, all at high-entropy decision points; causal oracle experiment proves it. RL-free entropy-gated contrastive FT matches or exceeds full RL across 3 model families × 6 scales × 6 math benchmarks at **3 orders of magnitude lower cost**. The post-training stack is up for re-examination. *(new, arXiv:2605.06241)*
2. **MemAgent** — ByteDance Seed / Tsinghua AIR, ICLR 2026 Oral. Trains an agent to overwrite a 1024-token plain-text memory buffer via Multi-Conv DAPO. 8K training context → 3.5M-token QA with <5% loss; >95% RULER 512K. No architectural change to base LLM. Third path between scaled attention and recurrent SSM. *(new — paper July 2025, trending now post-ICLR-2026, arXiv:2507.02259)*
3. **SST V2 (State Stream Transformer)** — Fifth Dimension. Per-layer FFN-driven nonlinear Latent State Cache streams horizontally across positions; two-pass parallel training of a nominally sequential dependency. **+15.15 pp GPQA-Diamond, 46% GSM8K error reduction** from a single-GPU QLoRA on Gemma-3 27B trained only on GSM8K. Beats DeepSeek V3 671B and Gemma 2.0 Flash with 25× fewer params. *(new, arXiv:2605.00206)*
