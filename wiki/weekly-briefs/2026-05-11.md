# Trends in the industry

- **Counter-tooling pipeline gives the wiki its first publisher-side adversarial-defense node, and the first cooperative-federation protocol with a reference implementation.** Two distinct counter-tool layers landed this week, both with releasable artefacts. **PIIGuard** (arXiv 2605.03129, May 4 2026) inverts indirect prompt injection from offensive primitive into a *publisher-side defensive* primitive — site owners embed hidden HTML that steers scraper-LLMs away from PII disclosure, with ≥97% direct-defense success in eval against GPT-5.4-nano / Claude-haiku-4.5 / DeepSeek-chat v3.2; code not yet linked, but threat model + mechanism are clean. **OpenCourier** (PCC + Princeton WAO, May 1 2026) ships a three-layer federation protocol (Registry / App-Instance / Instance-Requester) with reference multiplatform mobile app + admin dashboard at `opencourier.cs.princeton.edu` and arXiv 2511.02455 companion paper — the courier-facing analogue to Beckn (which is customer-vendor). Two new dedicated mechanism pages this run: [[mechanisms/adversarial-prompt-injection-defense]] and [[mechanisms/opencourier-protocol]]. *(synthesis)* The cooperative-substrate gap [[mechanisms/agent-interop-protocols]] was flagging — "what's the actual federation protocol that lets cooperative instances interoperate?" — now has a concrete first answer, scoped specifically to delivery / gig work.

- **Surveillance-pricing legislative cluster moves from "Maryland-first-mover" to a three-state architecture: prohibition (MD) vs. disclosure (NY) vs. infrastructure (CT).** Connecticut SB4 passed the House 141-6 on May 4 2026 (awaiting Lamont). It is qualitatively different from MD HB0895 and NY APDA: a **state-run data-broker registry + centralized one-click deletion mechanism spanning all registered brokers** (first cross-broker API-equivalent surface in US law, effective Jan 1 2027) plus a surveillance-pricing disclosure mandate for retail sellers + third-party delivery services. The wiki now carries a cross-state comparison table on [[counter-power/regulatory-responses]] mapping the three doctrinal theories: MD = first-in-nation prohibition (loophole-bound); NY = disclosure-with-AG-enforcement (Instacart behavioural-change capable); CT = disclosure + cross-broker deletion infrastructure (tooling-substrate capable). The "load-bearing first-in-nation prohibition" framing for HB0895 is now further qualified: CT broadens the picture from prohibition-vs-disclosure into prohibition-vs-disclosure-vs-infrastructure. Cross-state diffusion per Inside Privacy tracker: 40+ bills across 24 states YTD 2026.

- **JetBlue case becomes the wiki's first surveillance-pricing class action with named technical mechanism.** *Phillips v. The case opens the standing template for a "did this site price-discriminate against me?" audit tool — a Tier-1 [[strategies/data-disruption-strategy-map]] build candidate that now has explicit case-law standing precedent in motion.
