- **The $p(1-p)$ query-weight problem refuses to die.** MCPO (April 18) shows Dr. GRPO's std-removal fix does NOT eliminate the difficulty bias — the residual $p(1-p)$ weight persists and peaks at $p=0.5$ even after std is gone. MCPO fixes this with advantage-denominator rescaling + hinge-KL regularisation on mastered prompts. Directly relevant to the single-sample post-saturation regime flagged in [[research/rlvr-mechanics/_overview]] open questions — when your one prompt hits $p=1$, MCPO is a candidate answer to "what pulls the gradient now".

- **Curriculum-of-demonstrations is emerging as an alternative to Dynamic-Sampling** for the zero-variance exploration problem. CBRL (March 19) anneals few-shot demo prepending during RLVR — probability starts high to bootstrap exploration, decays to zero so the student must internalise without assistance. Directly maps onto [[research/synthesis/concept-curriculum-method]]'s per-concept packet schedule: CBRL supplies the concrete *schedule* that step (c) of the curriculum method left open.

- **Teacher-hint-design splitting into minimal-sufficient vs maximal-context camps.** KnowRL (April 2026) argues for atomic knowledge-point decomposition + Constrained Subset Search to find *minimal* sufficient guidance, uses NO KL loss, and claims more teacher context hurts. This contradicts Sakana RLT's design (full-solution-in-prompt + $r^{KL}$ plausibility loss), posing a direct hint-density question for [[research/synthesis/concept-curriculum-method]]'s packet construction — see the new conflict file [[conflicts/knowrl-vs-rlt-hint-design]].
