# Trends in the industry

- **Agent-to-service payments crossed from protocol-debate to managed product.** Amazon Bedrock AgentCore Payments (preview, 2026-05-17) ships a hyperscaler-native rail — HTTP 402 → x402 → USDC settlement on Base, session-level spend caps, Coinbase/Stripe wallets — into a field that already had open specs (Google AP2, ACP, MPP). The contested bit is AWS's "first managed" framing while it names AP2 as prior art; the load-bearing distinction is open-protocol vs. managed-platform (now tracked as conflict C21).
- **Anthropic widened enterprise distribution on two surfaces in one week** — Claude as the primary reasoning engine inside SAP's Joule/Business AI Platform (SAP Sapphire; forward-looking PR, no GA/pricing), and Claude Platform on AWS GA as an account-native managed-agent stack *distinct from Bedrock* where Anthropic — not AWS — is the data processor. This is wallet-share via OEM + hyperscaler channel, not a disclosed revenue print.
- **Frontier model vendors are verticalising into the services/SI layer.** OpenAI spun out a majority-owned ~$4B "Deployment Company" (~$10B pre-money; ~19 PE/consulting backers reported) and bought Tomoro for ~150 forward-deployed engineers — a direct answer to Google's $750M GSI fund and the Accenture-style channel. *(synthesis: the "agents eat services" thesis is being met by model vendors becoming the services firm rather than only selling the model.)*
- **MCP's unpatched security substrate is now a procurement variable, not a footnote.** SAP routing Claude through MCP makes it the 4th named incumbent confirming the "everything-as-MCP" norm (C17 Position A) while simultaneously inheriting the OX Security RCE surface (Position B). Lyrie.ai's Agent Trust Protocol ($2M pre-seed, IETF-bound, Anthropic CVP-accepted) is the first concrete protocol-level-isolation tooling datapoint — early and heavily caveated.
- **Practitioner backlash hardened into a coherent counter-signal** *(synthesis)*: Hashimoto's "AI Psychosis" (HN ~1,988), "AI is making me dumb" (HN ~541), Cursor's pricing-hike CEO apology, and an Ontario audit finding AI medical scribes "routinely blow basic facts" landed in the same week as the platform launches — a reliability-and-overreliance weight against the vendor cadence.
