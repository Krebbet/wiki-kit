# Trends in the industry

- **Pure RL (no distillation) is now a credible production recipe for reasoning models.** Magistral Medium reaches 73.6% AIME'24 pass@1 from a 26.8% base — almost closing the gap to DeepSeek-R1 (79.8%) — using only GRPO-based RL with no cold-start from external reasoning models. The five GRPO modifications Mistral settled on (no-KL, Clip-Higher ε_high=0.26–0.28, filtered zero-advantage groups, two-stage advantage norm, async 3-pool infra) are now the most battle-tested public specification for distillation-free reasoning RL. Combined with MAI-Thinking-1's announcement last week, this is two independent tier-1 labs arriving at the same conclusion: you don't need a teacher model to reach gold-medal math.

- **RL is moving upstream to pre-training.** RPT (Microsoft/PKU/THU) applies GRPO at the pre-training stage itself, treating each next-token prediction as an RL episode with a binary prefix-matching reward drawn from the corpus. Power-law scaling (R²≥0.96) across entropy difficulty tiers. This is structurally different from RLVR fine-tuning — the corpus is the verifier, no labeled data needed. If it generalizes beyond OmniMATH, it reframes the pre-training/post-training divide.

- **Token-level RL credit assignment keeps fragmenting.** Four distinct mechanistic frames now coexist in the wiki's sparse-policy-selection conflict (sparse selection, gradient cancellation, discriminator reweighting, spurious-reward/clipping bias). This week's DRPO (smooth quadratic regularizer vs hard ratio-clip) and Rewarding the Unlikely (unlikeliness reward to fix GRPO rank bias) are two more entries in the same cluster — the field is saturating with GRPO variants, each fixing a slightly different failure mode. The theoretical unification hasn't arrived yet.

- **Teaching ability is separable from reasoning ability.** RLT (Sakana AI, NeurIPS 2025) trains a 7B model to *explain* solutions to a student LM via RL — giving it the answer upfront and rewarding student comprehension directly. A 7B RLT teacher outperforms distillation from DeepSeek-R1 at <0.05% of R1's training compute. This is the clearest demonstration yet that the pedagogical quality of a distillation source is not determined by the teacher's own problem-solving score.

- **The RLVR debate is still unresolved, but the measurement problem is clearer.** The spurious-rewards finding (GRPO with random/wrong rewards still lifts Qwen2.5-Math by +21 pp) and the CoT-Pass@K resolution from last week jointly suggest the field has been measuring the wrong thing. Magistral's binary-vs-partial-credit ablation (partial reward: −2% LCB) is the first practitioner-confirmed data point that reward shaping *does* matter at the margin. The three frames are not mutually exclusive.
