# Trends in the industry

- **Individual DSAR is structurally broken; the fix is centralized collective infrastructure.** The first full empirical audit of California's data broker regime (522 brokers, FAccT 2026) found 9% full compliance, 43% blocking exercise of all privacy rights, and 64% injecting design friction. The legislative response — California DROP, and now Connecticut PA 26-64 (signed May 14, first state centralized one-stop deletion portal) — is converging on collective infrastructure rather than individual rights. The mechanism is shifting from "you can opt out" to "one request deletes from all." The gap between law-on-paper and infrastructure-in-practice is the build target.
- **Deployed differential privacy cannot be trusted without independent audit.** Apple's DifferentialPrivacy.framework — the largest shipped DP deployment in existence — has DP violations in 5 of 9 mechanisms covering 68–87% of collected data, per an IEEE S&P 2026 Distinguished Paper. Bugs known since 2012 are unpatched in production. Local DP is intentionally disabled in SecAgg protocols (confirmed by Apple). Epsilon values of 6–8 enable >99.75% membership inference. Apple reclassified the findings as "expected behaviour" after disclosure. Implication: any regulatory or compliance claim of DP protection is not meaningful without third-party audit.
- **Browser extension chokepoint is the live battleground for consumer counter-power.** uBlock Origin v1.71 is in an 8-week Firefox review queue; AdNauseam is Chrome-dead (MV3 makes its ad-click architecture impossible without ground-up rewrite); Privacy Badger's new GPC-default has a live signal bug. Chrome's MV3 transition is systematically degrading the API layer that consumer-side tools depend on. Browser vendors are the platform; the question is whether Firefox maintains parity.
- **Hub-and-spoke consent decree template is proven across two industries in one month.** DOJ-RealPage Tunney Act final approval (May 18) + Agri Stats consent decree (May 7): same five-component remedy structure (data-sharing prohibition, anonymisation floor, data-aging, open/non-discriminatory access, court monitor with code-review). Two completed decrees in rental housing and meat processing within 18 days. The template is now replicable — any hub-and-spoke data-aggregator-as-cartel-coordinator can be challenged on this model. Court monitors with algorithm-certification authority are the new accountability mechanism.
- **Evidence-access asymmetry is the primary bottleneck preventing AI harm litigation from clearing pre-trial.** The FAccT 2026 paper (Cen/Ismael/Zheng, CMU/Berkeley/Stanford) names seven structural barriers (model, data, documentation, logs, expertise, compute, infrastructure) that cause algorithmic harm cases to collapse before fact-finding. The proportionality test they propose — cause-of-action baseline + tailoring + less-restrictive alternative — is the first systematic framework courts can apply to AI discovery disputes. Fungibility insight: query access + operational logs + documentation can substitute for model weights in many audit contexts, meaning DSAR requests should be structured around the harm-reproduction minimum, not maximalist transparency demands.
- **Cooperative federation milestones stacking.** Open Food Network DFC API upgrade targeting June 2026 federated interoperability (cross-platform inventory sharing with Shopify and partners). Drivers Cooperative Colorado at 620+ driver-members, 12,000 riders, building "Driver Systems" as a federation infrastructure org. PCC 2026-2027 ICDE Fellowship cohort convened around "Solidarity Stack" federated digital infrastructure. Three distinct federation patterns — data interop layer, worker-owned rideshare scaling, and federated governance curriculum — all showing movement in the same 30-day window.
