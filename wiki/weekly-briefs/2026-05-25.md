# Trends in the industry
- **Agent-evaluation *validity* became the loudest academic thread this week (synthesis).** Six-plus papers in one week interrogate whether agent benchmarks and LLM-judges actually measure what they claim: evidence-bounded scoring that wraps existing benchmarks (*Can Agent Benchmarks Support Their Scores?*), LLM-judges hitting <55% on fine-grained failure detection (*Time to REFLECT*), span-level failure diagnosis that survives long traces (*Holistic Evaluation*), and a transparency audit of 12 benchmark papers. This is the same disease the wiki already named in the open [[conflicts/swe-bench-contamination]] and the LoCoMo-"ranking-flips-by-harness" finding — now generalising across the field.
- **Harness self-evolution pushed down to the source/runtime layer (synthesis).** Last quarter's self-evolution acted on skills and prompts (SkillOS, SDAR). This week [[patterns/moss-production-self-evolution]] rewrites the harness *source code* (routing/hooks/state) with a full production swap-and-rollback lifecycle, and *Adapting the Interface, Not the Model* adapts the runtime harness across 18 frozen backbones. The frontier is moving from "evolve the skill files" to "evolve the scaffold's code."
- **"Code as the substrate, not the artifact" crystallised as a framing.** A ~400-reference survey, [[patterns/code-as-agent-harness]], reframes the whole stack around code's executability / inspectability / statefulness, and now sits beside the wiki's [[patterns/externalization-survey]] as a second, orthogonal vocabulary anchor. Sharpest claim: elaborate adaptive multi-agent topologies are a *symptom* of a missing formal shared substrate, not an advance.
- **Long-context (and plain BM25) keeps beating dedicated memory on *knowledge* tasks.** [[memory/evomembench]] is the second matched benchmark this month (after [[memory/groupmembench]]) where extraction-based memory loses to a strong long-context LLM / BM25 on knowledge retrieval — but with a new wrinkle: extraction *does* stay competitive on *execution* tasks at tight context budgets. The verbatim-vs-extracted debate is hardening on the knowledge axis while splitting on a knowledge-vs-execution axis.
- **Agent security and agent-native infra both went production-vendor.** Uber's [[security/adr-uber-mcp-detection]] (10 months, 7,200 endpoints, 10k+ daily sessions) is the first production MCP-security detection system the wiki has tracked; Cursor ([[deployments/cursor-cloud-agents]]), Daytona, Railway, and LangChain all shipped agent-native cloud/runtime infrastructure in the same fortnight. Agent-compute and agent-security are becoming distinct product categories, not features.
