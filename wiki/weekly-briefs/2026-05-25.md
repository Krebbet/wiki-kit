# Trends in the industry
- **Recurrent-depth / latent-fixed-point reasoning had a breakout week.** GRAM (stochastic recursive reasoning, alphaXiv's top-scoring paper), HRM-Text (1B hierarchical-recurrent, beats Huginn 3.5B at 127× less compute), and Equilibrium Reasoners (reasoning as learned attractors, ~40k-equivalent-layer unrolls) all push the thesis that *depth-via-recurrence* — not parameter count or longer CoT — is the reasoning lever. The looped-transformer line ([[hyperloop-transformers]]) is graduating from a test-time-compute trick into a from-scratch architecture class. *(synthesis)*
- **Per-token RL credit assignment is consolidating into its own sub-field.** Three independent angles landed this week — AntiSD (a PMI identity showing default self-distillation *suppresses* deliberation tokens), DelTA (a discriminator view: shared/formatting tokens dominate the gradient centroids and dilute reward-separating directions), and Vector Policy Optimization (vector-valued rewards for solution diversity). All argue the bottleneck is the GRPO/DAPO *per-token weighting*, not the algorithm family. The wiki's open [[conflicts/sparse-policy-selection-vs-gradient-cancellation]] now has a third position. *(synthesis)*
- **Delta-rule linear attention reasserted itself over selective SSMs.** NVIDIA's Gated DeltaNet-2 beats Mamba-3 — the wiki's recurrent SOTA since April — at matched 1.3B scale by decoupling one scalar gate into channel-wise erase/write, and shows by ablation that fixed-state retrieval failures are an *update-rule* artifact, not a capacity ceiling. That reframes the long-running [[conflicts/fixed-state-ssm-long-context]] debate: the nearest-orthodox fixed-state defence finally arrived, and it wins by fixing the update, not enlarging the state.
- **On-policy distillation keeps compounding, and the consensus is "distill selectively, not uniformly."** Last week SDAR; this week AntiSD and HINT-SD. The recurring finding is that the *naïve* SD objective is actively harmful for reasoning, and the fixes converge — reverse the divergence (AntiSD), target failures via hindsight (HINT-SD), decouple direction from magnitude ([[rlsd-self-distilled-rlvr]]). *(synthesis)*
- **Efficiency work is migrating to the KV cache as the contested resource.** Full Attention Strikes Back (full→sparse in <100 steps, 9.36× prefill at 1M context), OScaR (near-lossless INT2 KV, 5.3× memory), and WorldKV (training-free KV for video world models) share a default assumption: attention is mostly sparse and mostly compressible — prove otherwise. *(synthesis)*

# Top 3 from the watchlist
1. **Equilibrium Reasoners: Learning Attractors Enables Scalable Reasoning** — reasoning recast as learned task-conditioned fixed-point attractors; latent fixed-point unrolling to ~40k-equivalent layers hits >99% on Sudoku-Extreme. The depth-scaling counterpart to this week's captured GRAM and HRM-Text. *(new, arXiv:2605.21488)*
2. **Vector Policy Optimization** — alphaXiv #1; a drop-in GRPO replacement that trains on vector-valued rewards to produce *diverse* solutions, lifting best-of-N and evolutionary-search budgets — a different lever on the per-token-RL debate than AntiSD/DelTA. *(new, arXiv:2605.22817)*
3. **Full Attention Strikes Back** — full-attention LLMs are intrinsically ~15%-sparse (retrieval heads); converts full→sparse in <100 training steps for 9.36× prefill at 1M context. The cheapest long-context efficiency lever surfaced this quarter. *(new, arXiv:2605.16928)*
