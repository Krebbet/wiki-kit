# Trends in the industry

- **Omnimodal unification is winning the world-model race.** NVIDIA Cosmos 3 — open weights, #1 across T2I/T2V/I2V/robot-policy — is the first model to unify language, image, video, audio, and action in a single architecture without explicit 3D scaffolds. Veo-3.1 (closed) is matched or beaten across most interactive benchmarks. The "pure video can't do interactive simulation" thesis (Moonlake) is now empirically challenged.

- **Agentic RL infrastructure is converging on a decoupled, harness-agnostic design.** Three independent papers this week (Polar/NVIDIA, ProRL Agent/Microsoft, Nemotron-Cascade 2/NVIDIA) arrived at near-identical conclusions: separate the RL rollout service from the training loop, treat the agent harness as a black box accessed via API proxy. This is the same design shift that happened to ML training infra 2015–2018 (distributed data pipelines decoupled from model training). Polar's +22.6pt SWE-Bench Verified gain with Qwen3.5-4B via Codex is the headline result.

- **The RLVR-vs-base-model debate has a formal resolution.** RLVR Incentivizes Reasoning (arXiv:2506.14245) proves that GRPO monotonically increases correct-CoT probability under a Logic Prior assumption, and shows that standard Pass@K is confounded on math benchmarks (base LLMs guess short integer answers). CoT-Pass@K reveals persistent RLVR advantage at all K up to 1024. The "sampling efficiency only" camp (Yue et al.) is reconciled — not refuted, just metric-invalidated. Five distinct mechanistic positions now coexist in the sparse-policy-selection conflict.

- **Muon optimizer is going mainstream.** The mechanistic explanation for why Muon beats Adam (lower Normalized Directional Sharpness on attention/FFN weights) landed this week alongside NorMuon variants. DeepSeek V4 adopted Muon at 1.6T-param MoE scale — the first production deployment at this size. Combined with Polar Express (ICLR HM) already on the watchlist, Muon is past the "interesting research idea" phase.

- **DeepSeek V4 sets a new bar for open-source coding models.** CSA+HCA attention achieves 10% of V3's KV cache at 1M context; LiveCodeBench 93.5 (best reported), Codeforces 3206, SWE-Verified 80.6 (matching Claude Opus 4.6). Also first production deployment of mHC from the wiki's manifold-constrained-hyper-connections page. Flash-Max (13B active) nearly matches Pro-Max (49B active) on reasoning tasks — a significant active-parameter efficiency result.

- **Distillation-free reasoning is now a credible production claim.** MAI-Thinking-1 (Microsoft, 35B active/~1T MoE, 97% AIME 2025, zero synthetic/distilled data) challenges the dominant mid-size reasoning model recipe (DeepSeek-R1 distill / teacher-model CoT harvesting). No paper or weights yet, but the Build 2026 announcement carries weight from a tier-1 lab. If confirmed, it weakens the case for the distillation-first stack many practitioners are currently building on.
