# Trends in the industry

- **Agentic AI is crossing from extraction tool to consumer-side repair infrastructure.** DPAgent-in-the-Middle (arXiv 2606.06914) deploys an LLM as an active DOM-rewriting browser proxy that detects and undoes dark patterns on live websites — the same AI capability platforms use to automate manipulation, inverted. 98% of 485 real sites tested contain dark patterns; 77% of those patterns are automatically repaired. The paper also formalises "AI grooming": adversaries exploiting data voids to poison LLM training pipelines with semantically benign but ground-truth-deceptive UI samples — a new supply-chain attack vector flowing in the opposite direction. *(synthesis: this week's capture + last week's healthcare dark-patterns findings together indicate dark-pattern automation is accelerating on both sides simultaneously.)*

- **Obfuscation research is converging on a single design principle: stochastic on-manifold substitution defeats purification adversaries; deterministic substitution does not.** GHOST (arXiv 2606.03711) proves this for location data — the third domain (after images via Nightshade, and differential-privacy text via the May 2026 DP text obfuscation paper) where this pattern has been demonstrated this year. The field is reaching a result: randomised, plausibility-constrained substitution is the necessary design primitive for obfuscation tools to survive adversarial deployment.

- **Identity infrastructure is emerging as the third dark-pattern frontier.** The EUDI Wallet credential oversharing paper (arXiv 2606.06354) documents the same structural playbook as healthcare data intake (Phreesia, May 2026) and data broker DSAR (FAccT 2026, May 2025): make the consent mechanism confusing, benefit from over-disclosure. At EUDI scale (~700M EU residents) the oversharing surface is larger than either of the two prior sectors. The "Credential Assistant" counter-tool introduces a new mechanism: collective crowdsourced disclosure-frequency norms as automated guidance — a data-cooperative pattern applied to identity rather than pricing data.

- **The ILO platform worker Convention creates the first binding specification for algorithmic management transparency.** The first-ever global labour standard for gig-economy platform workers — covering algorithmic management transparency + appeal rights, employment classification, social protection portability, and collective bargaining — is being finalised this week at ILC-114. The algorithmic transparency and appeal provisions define what platform audit tooling needs to verify: a compliance surface analogous to what the RealPage consent decree's court-monitor regime does for pricing algorithms in the US.

- **Surveillance infrastructure fusion is broadening the anti-surveillance toolkit's target surface.** SignalTrace (404 Media, June 8) turns ALPR cameras from vehicle-trackers into person-trackers by sweeping Bluetooth device identifiers of phones, AirPods, and wearables in passing cars. 40K+ ALPR cameras are already deployed across 24 US states (BusPatrol); adding SignalTrace to that fleet is a qualitative expansion of location-surveillance infrastructure. The counter-toolkit for this (MAC address randomisation, Bluetooth proximity management, device-ID scrubbing) is scattered and non-integrated — an open tooling gap.
