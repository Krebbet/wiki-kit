# Trends in the industry

- **RLVR legitimacy crisis deepening.** The "Spurious Rewards" result (GRPO with random rewards yields 73% of real-reward gains on Qwen family) has forced a four-way split on why RL works at all: sparse policy selection (ReasonMaxxer), gradient cancellation (DFPO), discriminative token contrast (DelTA), or clipping-bias amplification of pretrained behaviors (Spurious Rewards). No consensus position holds cleanly; the debate is model-family-dependent, which complicates cross-lab benchmark comparisons.
- **Entropy-selective RL is becoming the operative framework.** The NeurIPS 2025 forking-token paper (Beyond the 80/20 Rule) closes the loop from ReasonMaxxer's post-hoc observation to an active training-time intervention — gradient-mask the 80% low-entropy tokens, get +11 pp at 32B scale. This, combined with AntiSD and Delta-Token-Credit, establishes entropy-gated token selection as the emerging consensus mechanism regardless of which causal account is correct.
- **Text-space self-improvement emerging as a distinct paradigm.** SkillOpt (skill documents), SEAL (self-generated fine-tuning data + hyperparameters via RL), and the earlier GEPA/EvoSkill line establish a spectrum: prompt-space → skill-document-space → weight-space self-modification, each with distinct optimization machinery. SkillOpt's zero-inference-overhead result and cross-model skill transfer are the most practically actionable finding this week.
- **RL systems infrastructure maturing.** LlamaRL's 10.7× async speedup at 405B, the Straggler-Aware Group Sizing paper (synchronous RL within 15% of async), and the existing "Accelerating RL Post-Training Rollouts via Speculative Decoding" watchlist entry form a coherent emerging stack: the bottleneck has shifted from algorithm to pipeline throughput. Production-scale RLVR is no longer just a frontier lab problem.
- **Diffusion LMs gaining theory and training-time techniques.** LoopMDM loops early DiT layers in masked diffusion (3.3× fewer FLOPs to match standard MDM), joining the looped-transformer line's extension to generation. The Discrete Diffusion survey (arXiv:2506.13759) and the Break-the-Block RL paper (watchlist) confirm dLLMs are past the "interesting but impractical" phase.
- **Self-improving agents diversifying axes of modification.** HGM (code), SEAL (weights), SkillOpt (skill docs), BES (trajectory search), SDAR/HINT-SD (multi-turn distillation) — five distinct self-improvement vectors are being explored simultaneously. The community hasn't converged on which axis matters most for real-world capability gains.
