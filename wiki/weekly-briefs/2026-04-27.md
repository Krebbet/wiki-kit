- **Driver-cooperative model going national-and-state-simultaneously, with a new gov-coop hybrid lens.** **Bharat Taxi** launched in Mumbai April 23 under India's Multi-State Co-op Societies Act with 5.17 lakh drivers nationally, ~25K rides/day, zero-commission claim, EV procurement and ₹5 lakh insurance bundling, passenger services slated for May. **Drivers Cooperative Colorado** is awaiting a decision on its RTD public-transit contract bid. Two distinct political economies; same structural bet. The Bharat Taxi case **doesn't fit the wiki's existing OECD four-type platform-coop typology** — state-sponsored under cooperative law with state capital substituting for member capital. Logged as a new typological category on [[mechanisms/platform-cooperatives]]; new page [[organizations/bharat-taxi]].
- **EU DMA produces the week's clearest new data-access surface.** April 16 preliminary findings under Article 6(11) propose mandating that Google share **ranking, query, click, and view data** with third-party search engines on FRAND terms. AI chatbots are explicitly named as eligible data beneficiaries. Public consultation closes May 1; final decision expected July 27. If finalised, this is the first time commercial search-intent data is regulated-access-shared — a candidate substrate for [[strategies/mechanism-synthesis-readout|Build #1 (Federated Pricing Observatory)]] which currently has no clean way to obtain grounded search-intent signals.
