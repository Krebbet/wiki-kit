# Trends in the industry

- **Looped Transformers are converging into a real architecture family.** Four near-simultaneous April submissions land in the same cluster: [[hyperloop-transformers]] (loop-level mHC, beats depth-matched at 50% params), ELT (elastic visual generative loops, FID 2.0 ImageNet at 4× fewer params), Loop-Think-Generalize (recurrent depth for compositional generalization), Universal Transformers Need Memory (depth-vs-state trade-off theory). Universal Transformers (Dehghani 2018) and ALBERT-style param sharing have a name now: "loop-as-test-time-compute". Granularity matters — Hyperloop's headline contribution is *where* you apply hyper-connections (loop boundaries, not layers), not the loop itself.
- **GRPO is being fixed structurally rather than replaced.** [[token-gradient-cancellation]] gives the GRPO-line a formal failure mode (gradient exchangeability) and a 1-line stop-gradient fix (DFPO). [[neural-garbage-collection]] extends GRPO's *action space* to include KV-cache eviction. TEPO (2604.12736) is a third concurrent GRPO-line fix. The mode of activity has shifted: a year ago every paper *replaced* GRPO; this week three papers *fix* it. (synthesis)
- **Evolution-strategies camp now spans weight space *and* prompt space.** [[gepa-reflective-prompt-evolution]] (ICLR 2026 oral) beats GRPO with **35× fewer rollouts**, no weight updates — the same competitive claim [[eggroll]] makes from weight-space ES. [[conflicts/grpo-vs-evolution-strategies]] now has Position-A entrants on two axes (prompts, weights). Pareto-illumination + reflective mutation + system-aware merge crossover is the new combination; the result *reverses* Wan et al. 2024's instruction-vs-few-shot finding.
- **The KV-cache-compression line is splitting along a "trained vs training-free" axis.** [[triattention]] (last week) is *training-free* pre-RoPE pruning; [[neural-garbage-collection]] (this week) is *end-to-end RL-trained* eviction. Both target the same bottleneck; opposite tradeoffs (no fine-tuning vs task-specific RL). SnapKV, KeyDiff, KNorm, StreamingLLM are now squarely the "fixed-heuristic baselines" in both papers.
- **SSM-vs-associative-memory framing is a real disagreement, not just vocabulary.** [[mamba-3]] §5.4 explicitly argues complex-state SSM dynamics aren't expressible inside MIRAS's regression-coefficient frame — the load-bearing example is that *complex values are meaningless as regression coefficients but coherent as SSM transitions*. Opened [[conflicts/ssm-vs-associative-memory-taxonomy]] this week. Distinct from the empirical [[conflicts/fixed-state-ssm-long-context]] dispute. (synthesis)
- **The "fixed-state SSM at long context" debate has shifted from *can it work?* to *only with attention layers?*** Mamba-3 *concedes* the retrieval weakness of pure fixed-state SSMs (§4.1.2) and recommends hybrid Mamba-3+attention. Pure-vs-hybrid is the new cut. Position-A camp ([[titans-miras]] / [[nested-learning]]) still wins on pure SSMs; hybrids are the live battleground.
- **ICLR 2026 is the week's main signal source.** Multi-signal weight came from ICLR oral / poster acceptance, not preprint date — Mamba-3 (March), GEPA (July 2025) trended in this window because the conference is happening. Worth keeping in mind for the next /weekly-brief: conference-week signal will skew older arXiv IDs upward.

# Top 3 from the watchlist

1. **Mamba-3** — Mamba-2 successor with three SSM-principled deltas (exp-trapezoidal discretization, complex-valued state transitions, MIMO matmul updates); +1.8 avg over Mamba-2 at 1.5B; first selective SSM with complex state, solving TC⁰-hard parity / modular-arithmetic tasks Mamba-2 cannot. *(new, arXiv:2603.15569 / ICLR 2026 poster)*
2. **GEPA: Reflective Prompt Evolution** — Genetic-Pareto prompt optimizer that beats GRPO by up to 20% with up to 35× fewer rollouts; cross-model transfer (Qwen3-8B-optimized prompts beat GPT-4.1-Mini-native baselines). *(new, arXiv:2507.19457 / ICLR 2026 oral)*
3. **Token Gradient Cancellation / DFPO** — Formal "gradient exchangeability" condition for stable GRPO-style RL; two minimal stop-gradient transforms restore cancellation. +5–7 pp over GSPO at Qwen3-32B / 80B-MoE; gain grows with group size. *(new, arXiv:2604.13088)*
