# RLCSD: Reinforcement Learning with Contrastive On-Policy Self-Distillation

RLCSD (arXiv:2606.11709, Alibaba/Tsinghua) fixes a structural failure mode in On-Policy Self-Distillation (OPSD): when a correct-hint $h^+$ is injected as privileged context, the hinted model produces shorter, more direct outputs, so the KL gap $D_{KL}(p_\theta(\cdot | x, h^+) \| p_\theta(\cdot | x))$ concentrates on *style tokens* rather than task-bearing reasoning tokens — "privilege-induced style drift." RLCSD cancels this by subtracting the KL gap under a wrong hint $h^-$, which captures the pure style shift induced by conditioning on any hint regardless of correctness, leaving a signal concentrated on what the correct hint uniquely provides. The method consistently outperforms GRPO and prior OPSD variants across Qwen3 (1.7B/4B/8B) and OLMo-3-7B-Think on math and logical reasoning, and plugs in as a drop-in improvement to existing OPSD-family methods. Relevance to this wiki: contrastive subtraction is the first mechanism shown to make dense token-level OPD supervision *semantically targeted*, directly addressing the concern (raised by KnowRL) that maximal hints degrade signal quality without needing to reduce hint richness.

## Method

Standard OPSD supervises the student with the KL gap between its own hinted and unhinted distributions:

$$\mathcal{L}_{OPSD} = D_{KL}(p_\theta(\cdot \mid x, h^+) \| p_\theta(\cdot \mid x))$$

where $h^+$ is a verified correct solution injected as prefix context, and both distributions come from the same model $p_\theta$ (self-distillation). The pathology: conditioning on any solution hint, correct or not, causes the model to produce shorter, more direct completions. This style shift is hint-induced, not correctness-induced, so the KL gap misattributes style tokens as high-signal supervision.

RLCSD introduces a wrong-hint baseline $h^-$ (a plausible-but-incorrect solution) and takes the contrastive difference:

$$\mathcal{L}_{RLCSD} = D_{KL}(p_\theta(\cdot \mid x, h^+) \| p_\theta(\cdot \mid x)) - D_{KL}(p_\theta(\cdot \mid x, h^-) \| p_\theta(\cdot \mid x))$$

The second term estimates the style-shift component common to any hint conditioning; subtracting it isolates the correctness-specific signal. Both hinted rollouts are generated on-policy at each training step, so the method remains fully on-policy. No new hyperparameters are introduced beyond the choice of $h^-$ generation procedure.

The contrastive objective is modular: it extends to cross-model OPD (where the hinted distribution comes from a separate teacher) by applying the same subtraction, making it a drop-in improvement to any OPSD-family method.

## Results

The paper reports results across 9 tables and 9 figures (20 pages). Tested models: Qwen3-1.7B, Qwen3-4B, Qwen3-8B, OLMo-3-7B-Think. RLCSD outperforms GRPO and prior OPSD baselines at all four model sizes on mathematical and logical reasoning benchmarks. The contrastive plug-in also improves existing OPSD-family baselines when applied to them, suggesting privilege-induced style drift is pervasive across the family rather than specific to vanilla OPSD.

## Sample efficiency

RLCSD is on-policy and makes no claim to reduced sample count; the method is a training-loop modification, not a data-reduction technique. Per-step cost is approximately 2x vanilla OPSD: two hinted rollouts (correct + wrong) are generated per training sample rather than one. No off-policy replay or large dataset is required. The method's relevance to this wiki's thesis is signal quality per sample, not reduced sample count — contrastive subtraction extracts more task-relevant information from each training example by denoising the supervision target.

## Limitations

- Source of $h^-$ at scale is underspecified: the paper does not fully detail whether wrong hints are generated by rejection sampling from $p_\theta$, from a separate model, or from a fixed error bank.
- 2x per-step rollout cost vs vanilla OPSD.
- Experiments limited to math and logical reasoning; generalization to open-ended or multi-turn tasks is future work.
- Cross-model OPD extension receives less ablation depth than the self-distillation variant.
- The style-drift diagnosis (signal concentrates on style tokens) is asserted from analysis but the specific attribution mechanism is not ablated in depth.
