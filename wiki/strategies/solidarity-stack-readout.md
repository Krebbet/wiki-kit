# Solidarity Stack — Strategy Readout

*(strategy layer — editorial / synthesis.)*

Strategy-layer readout of the **Solidarity Stack** framework named by **Trebor Scholz** (Platform Cooperativism Consortium, The New School) and **Morshed Mannan** in Scholz's keynote at the **Cooperative AI Conference, Istanbul, November 11 2025** (PCC's 11th annual convening, attended by ~30 countries; published as PCC blog post Apr–May 2026). The framework names a **seven-layer cooperatively-owned counter-architecture for AI** spanning from raw materials to global governance, plus a federation mechanism (**Solidarity Stack Circles** → 2026 global online course → democratic planetary organization) explicitly modelled on **SWIFT** as a working example of a global cooperative protocol layer.

This readout maps each Solidarity Stack layer to existing wiki anchors, notes where the framework loads or contests current strategy positions, and flags the named real-world instances that warrant follow-up `/research` runs.

## Why this framework loads the wiki

The wiki has accumulated mechanism pages — [[mechanisms/platform-cooperatives]], [[mechanisms/data-cooperatives]], [[mechanisms/federated-learning]], [[mechanisms/agent-interop-protocols]], [[mechanisms/decentralized-agent-identity]], [[mechanisms/collective-bargaining-for-data]] — without a single cross-layer narrative architecture connecting them. [[strategies/mechanism-synthesis-readout]] is the closest cross-cutting synthesis, but treats the mechanisms as building blocks for novel *builds*, not as a layered *stack*. Scholz's framing is **complementary**: it provides a vertical structure (Earth → Compute → Data → Algorithms → Labour → Application → Governance) into which the wiki's mechanism pages already fit, and surfaces a federation primitive (Solidarity Stack Circles) that operationalises the wiki's Tier-1 strategy bets.

The framework is **rhetorical-but-mechanism-bearing** — a keynote address, editorial in register, but with named layers and named real-world instances at every layer. Treat it as strategy-layer synthesis, not reference. Its load-bearing contributions to this wiki:

1. **A cross-layer architecture** that ties the wiki's mechanism pages into a single counter-stack narrative.
2. **A federation primitive** (Circles → global course → democratic organization) that gives strategy-layer concreteness to the federation pattern documented at [[organizations/coopcycle]].
3. **Named instances at each layer** that are concrete `/research` candidates.
4. **A diagnostic claim** — *"the main problem … is not technological, it is a collective action problem"* — that is consistent with the wiki's mandate framing but pushes weight more forcefully toward bottom-up cooperative infrastructure than current pages do.

## Federation mechanism — Circles → Course → Organization

Scholz's concrete next step is a **Solidarity Stack Circles** programme: small working groups seeded at the November 2025 conference, each scoping a specific layer of the stack, federating into a **2026 global online course** that — explicitly modelled on SWIFT's interbank-protocol cooperative — produces a **democratic planetary organisation** capable of coordinating cross-layer governance.

This is the **strategy-layer concrete artefact** of the keynote. Two readings:

- **As implementation milestone**: the 2026 global course is a watchable signal; if it generates a federation-level governance vehicle, that vehicle becomes a candidate counterparty for the wiki's Build #1 (Federated Pricing Observatory) and Build #2 (Verifiable Mandate-Bound DSAR Pipeline) on [[strategies/mechanism-synthesis-readout]].
- **As wiki research candidate**: the Circles programme participants and the course curriculum (when published) are candidate sources for next-quarter `/research` runs on cooperative federation governance.

**SWIFT analogy as design-input for [[mechanisms/agent-interop-protocols]].** SWIFT (Society for Worldwide Interbank Financial Telecommunication) is a member-owned cooperative founded 1973, currently ~11,000 financial-institution members across 200+ countries, processing global interbank messaging on a cooperatively-governed protocol. As a precedent for a planetary cooperative-governance model over an interop protocol, SWIFT meaningfully extends the wiki's existing federation precedents ([[organizations/coopcycle]] at 72 coops / 12 countries; [[organizations/mondragon]] at ~70K workers / 60 years). SWIFT does what Scholz wants for AI infrastructure: a planetary-scale cooperative governing a critical-infrastructure protocol with member democratic accountability.

## Conflict / contestation flags

Scholz frames regulation as *"indispensable, but not enough"* and is explicitly skeptical of EU-level regulatory bets: *"The EU Stack project comes too late — and rests on the fiction that policymakers are uniformly leftist and ready to act."*

This is **mild tension** with the wiki's regulatory-proxy lever framing on [[counter-power/regulatory-responses]] and [[strategies/possible-strategic-levers]]. The wiki currently treats regulation (DMA, DSA, state-level surveillance-pricing prohibitions) as a live counter-power lane. The Solidarity Stack frame does not deny the wiki's regulatory-proxy position but **shifts weight more aggressively toward bottom-up cooperative infrastructure-building** than current pages do.

The Pluralistic HB0895 loophole assessment captured in this same week's brief (extends [[counter-power/regulatory-responses]] § 3) **partially corroborates** Scholz's skepticism — Maryland's first-in-nation prohibition law has carve-outs that materially weaken its enforcement reach. Together, the two sources push toward: *regulation is necessary scaffolding for cooperative-infrastructure builds, not a substitute for them.* This is a strategy-layer position, not a reference-layer claim.

**No `wiki/conflicts/` file warranted** — Scholz's framing is editorial / strategic disagreement, not a falsifiable empirical contradiction with any reference-layer claim.

## Diagnostic claims to track

Scholz makes three diagnostic claims worth flagging for empirical follow-up:

1. **AI is a vertically integrated extractive stack** — *"hardware, cloud infrastructure, models, labor, and applications, each layer concentrated in the hands of a few firms."* Quantification cited: Nvidia market cap exceeding Germany's GDP. **Follow-up:** is this concentration getting more or less pronounced quarter-over-quarter? Worth a future `/research` candidate.
2. **Stack outages have civilian-life consequences** — AWS outages locking people out of homes (smart locks tied to AWS-hosted authentication); a South Korean data-centre fire erasing 800 TB of public records. **Follow-up:** these are problem-framing examples that load the case for community-data-centre cooperatives (Solidarity Stack layer 2). Worth tracking as the failure-mode catalogue for centralised-cloud dependency.
3. **The bottleneck is not technical but a collective-action problem.** This is the framing that aligns most directly with the wiki's mandate. The diagnostic is consistent with the **complex-contagion** result on [[mechanisms/complex-contagion]] — collective action requires multi-reinforcement, not single-broadcaster propagation — and with the **issuer-governance unsolved** problem flagged on [[mechanisms/decentralized-agent-identity]]. Both are framings of "technology is solved, governance is not."

## Strategic implications for the wiki

*(editorial.)*

1. **Use the Solidarity Stack as the cross-layer narrative architecture in the wiki's strategy layer.** The strategy-layer index ([[strategies/index]]) currently presents readouts as a flat list. Adding a Solidarity Stack overlay — a section on the index that maps existing strategy-layer pages onto the seven layers — would give a reader a single mental model for *where in the stack* each lever / build / readout operates.
2. **Three new research candidates surfacing in this run.** (a) **African Tech Worker Cooperative** (data-labour worker-coop layer, post-Meta/Sama lawsuit lineage) — research-queue Priority 2 candidate. (b) **Sicredi Ouro Verde / Arla Foods** (cooperatives adopting AI on cooperative terms) — research-queue Priority 3 candidate. (c) **Hostsharing eG / Ashton Data Center Cooperative** (community data-centre layer) — research-queue Priority 3 candidate.
3. **Track the 2026 global course.** If the Circles programme produces a published curriculum, member roster, or federation governance proposal, those become primary sources for the wiki. Add to [[watchlist]] under "Forward-looking themes."
4. **Build #1 (Federated Pricing Observatory)** on [[strategies/mechanism-synthesis-readout]] gains a candidate cooperative counterparty if the Circles programme produces a planetary-scale federation. Currently Build #1's open question is *who is the operating cooperative?* The Solidarity Stack federation is a candidate answer, watchable on the timescale of late-2026.
5. **The SWIFT analogy** loads [[mechanisms/agent-interop-protocols]] with a working planetary-scale precedent for cooperative protocol governance. Update that page in the next refresh to cite SWIFT as the long-running cooperative-protocol-cooperative example, not just as financial-messaging infrastructure.

## Honest limits of this readout

- **Single-source.** The Solidarity Stack framework is from one keynote / blog post by one author (with a co-developer credited). Cross-corroboration with Mannan's academic work + the published Circles curriculum is a future research need.
- **Rhetorical register.** The keynote uses motivational framing (a Queens-cab-driver suicide vignette opens it). The wiki should cite the layered architecture and the named instances; the rhetorical register itself does not belong in the reference layer.
- **No falsifiable mechanism claims.** The Solidarity Stack is a normative framework, not an empirical-testing programme. The wiki should not treat it as evidence; it is a strategy-layer organising lens.
- **EU-skepticism may be miscalibrated for this wiki.** The wiki's reference layer documents EU enforcement actions (DMA, DSA, EU DMA Google FRAND access on [[counter-power/regulatory-responses]]) that are operating in real-time; Scholz's framing under-weights what those mechanisms have actually shipped this year.
