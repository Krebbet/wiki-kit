# Time Banks and LETS

**Time-based currency** systems — most commonly operating as **time banks** (TimeBanks.org) and through the **hOurworld international network** — implement [[possible-strategic-levers|strategic lever #20 (Time-bank / Local Exchange Trading System)]]: consumers trade labour-hours directly, bypassing the monetary pricing layer entirely. The mechanism sidesteps dynamic pricing, pricing-algorithm personalisation, and broker-layer extraction simultaneously — not by countering them, but by not transacting in currency where the algorithms operate.

## Mechanism (per captured TimeBanks.org primary source)

Core rules:

- **1 hour of time = 1 Time Dollar.** One-for-one, regardless of service provided or skill required.
- Each exchange is recorded as a **corresponding credit and debit** in member accounts.
- Time Dollars do not earn interest and are not subject to inflation.
- Credits can be saved indefinitely.

From the captured TimeBanks.org source, the design goals of the flat-rate structure:

> Designed to both recognize and encourage reciprocal community service, resist inflation, avoid hoarding, enable trade, and encourage cooperation among participants.

## Live networks

- **TimeBanks.org (TimeBanks USA).** US-focused network.
- **hOurworld®.** International directory of community timebanks, running on the "Time and Talents" software platform. Per the captured WebSearch preamble: "members earn time credits sharing their talents and services, then later spend those credits on services others provide, strengthening the fabric of local communities."

## Common exchanged services (per captured source)

From the TimeBanks.org capture: tutoring, childcare, home repairs, cooking, transportation, companionship. Savings per participant, self-reported: **$300–$1,200 per year** by exchanging these services.

## Counter-power mechanism

Time banks are a **true disintermediation lever** ([[possible-strategic-levers|lever #20]]). Structural properties:

1. **Transactions never become priced in currency.** Pricing-algorithm extraction is impossible on a transaction that does not involve money. No dynamic-pricing surface, no personalisation, no broker layer.
2. **No marketplace operator capturing margin.** Unlike [[open-food-network|Open Food Network]]'s 1% fee or [[drivers-cooperative|The Drivers Cooperative]]'s 15%, a time bank has no transaction-volume-based revenue at all. Operating costs are covered by membership fees, grants, or volunteer infrastructure.
3. **Inflation-resistance by design.** A skilled professional's hour and an unskilled helper's hour are valued equally — not because their market values are equal, but because the time-bank design *rejects market valuation as the pricing axis*. This is a deliberate departure from monetary markets.

## Scale and limits

- **Small per-member dollar value.** Reported $300–$1,200/yr per member is meaningful but non-dominant for most members' economic activity. Time banks complement, not replace, monetary exchange.
- **Category-bound.** Most exchanges are services, not goods. Capital-intensive needs (housing, medical care, durable goods) are not typically exchangeable through time banks.
- **Community-scale friction.** Time banks require a critical mass of local members with complementary skill sets. Works best in dense urban neighbourhoods or tight-knit communities.
- **Not a pricing-counter tool.** Time banks do not address pricing-algorithm extraction in markets where members are still buying from corporate sellers. They are a partial exit, not a full counter.

## Relevance for pricing-counter strategy

*(editorial / synthesis.)*

Time banks occupy a structural position no other mechanism on this wiki does. Every other counter-power mechanism (transparency tools, data coops, platform coops, consumer coops, GPOs, boycott apps, consumer unions, CCAs) operates *within* the monetary market — correcting power asymmetries, redistributing margin, making opaque behaviour visible. Time banks operate *outside* the monetary market for the categories they cover.

Implications for a strategic portfolio:

1. **Full exit where feasible, counter-power where not.** Time banks work for categories where labour-hour-equivalence is a plausible valuation (service-to-service, neighbour-to-neighbour). They do not work for purchasing durable goods or accessing capital-intensive services. A strategic portfolio that combines time-bank adoption in the service layer with data-coop or pricing-observatory defence in the goods layer would address more of consumer-extraction surface than either alone.
2. **Sustainability model comparison.** Time banks have **no transaction revenue** — the sustainability challenge is pure organising + membership-fees + grants. This is the inverse extreme of [[paypal-honey|Honey's affiliate-commission-captured model]] that drifts toward extraction. Neither extreme is safe: Honey drifts into extraction; time banks struggle to scale. [[midata|MIDATA]]'s open-source + cooperative-membership + grant model sits between the two.
3. **Data-footprint implication.** A transaction on a time bank produces no data for a dynamic-pricing algorithm to train on. Collective migration of service transactions to time banks is a form of [[obfuscation|data-footprint reduction]] distinct from obfuscation-by-counterfeit-data: obfuscation by absence rather than by noise.
