# The Drivers Cooperative

The Drivers Cooperative is a worker-owned ridesharing company and mobile app launched in New York City in May 2021, operating as a direct alternative to Uber and Lyft. It is the most prominent US platform cooperative in the rideshare sector and serves as a concrete counter-power example against the dynamic-pricing / gig-economy model documented on [[consumer-facing-dynamic-pricing]]. It sits within the broader [[platform-cooperatives]] movement.

## Structure and economics

- **Ownership:** drivers are members; each driver owns one share and one vote in business and leadership decisions (per Wikipedia, captured via the [[platform-cooperatives]] references).
- **Commission:** the cooperative takes **15% from each ride** for business overhead, compared to **25–40%** for Uber and Lyft (per Wikipedia entry on The Drivers Cooperative).
- **Pay uplift:** drivers.coop states that members "make 8–10% more on each trip than on Uber and Lyft."
- **Profit distribution:** after loans and expenses, remaining profit is returned to drivers as weighted dividends.
- **Current scope** (per drivers.coop primary source): driver-owned cooperative in New York City, specialising in **paratransit and Non-Emergency Medical Transportation (NEMT)**. The primary source describes current expansion as "working hard to bring driver-owned rideshare to your city" via app-based recruitment.

## Founding team and theory of the case

Per the Grassroots Economic Organizing (GEO) excerpt:

- **Ken Lewis** — co-founder, Grenadan immigrant and for-hire vehicle driver.
- **Erik Forman** — co-founder; background is 15 years in labour organising (fast food industry; his own high school when he was a teacher in NYC), then labour educator with the **Independent Drivers Guild** (a union representing 80,000+ for-hire drivers in NYC; receives some funding from Uber).
- **Alissa Orlando** — co-founder; former Uber executive.

**Forman on the cost side of the business model:** "Half of every dollar drivers make is eaten by vehicle expenses. If we're looking to increase pay, it makes sense to focus on both sides of the problem."

**Forman on profitability:** to break even, the cooperative needs to complete about 1,300 trips per day. NYC has 400,000+ daily rideshare trips. "We only need to claim a small sliver of the market to have a self-sustaining operation."

**Forman on the savings from not waging political war:** "If you're not trying to bankroll an assault on workers rights in the United States, it turns out you save a lot of money."

## Positioning claim

From the drivers.coop homepage (Ken Lewis): *"While Uber and Lyft make their money for Wall Street and Silicon Valley investors, we are a co-operative…. profits go back to the drivers."*

Mission statement from the homepage: to build the "100% worker-owned transportation company of the future."

## Counter-power mechanism

This is a textbook **exit-pathway** counter-power action: rather than regulating or litigating the incumbent platforms (see [[regulatory-responses]]), The Drivers Cooperative builds a parallel institution with different ownership. It complements, rather than substitutes for, the DOJ/FTC/state-AG regulatory responses covered elsewhere in the wiki.

**Relationship to the dynamic-pricing critique:** Uber's surge pricing (documented on [[consumer-facing-dynamic-pricing]]) extracts value from riders and transmits it mostly to investors; Drivers Cooperative's flat 15% fee and driver-dividend structure attempts to redistribute the same transaction flow toward workers. The co-op does not directly address algorithmic pricing toward riders — its theory of change is on the supply (driver) side.

## Scope and limitations

- **Paratransit / NEMT focus:** the cooperative's current primary service category is specialised medical transportation rather than general-purpose rideshare. This is a contestable-market choice consistent with Sundararajan's conditions for cooperative viability (low worker-diversity, limited external competition, lower rate of technology change) — see discussion on [[platform-cooperatives]].
- **Thin capture of the primary org source.** The drivers.coop homepage is a single-page app; only the static content (mission, key figures, Ken Lewis quote) captured. The full app experience and current operational metrics are not documented here.
- **No independent financial reporting yet captured.** Founding-team claims (8–10% pay uplift, 15% commission) are self-reported; no independent audit has been captured.
