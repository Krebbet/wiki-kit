# Driver's Seat Cooperative

Driver's Seat Cooperative is a **US driver-owned data cooperative** for rideshare and delivery workers, incorporated in 2019 as a Limited Cooperative Association (LCA). Distinct from [[drivers-cooperative|The Drivers Cooperative]] (an NYC worker-owned rideshare *platform*). Driver's Seat pooled driver-generated data to produce income-optimisation insights for drivers and mobility insights sold to city agencies. **The coop appears to have sunset:** the driversseat.co homepage returns a Squarespace "account expired" page as of 2026-04-21, and activity has transitioned to the Workers' Algorithm Observatory (WAO) at Princeton *(editorial observation based on capture)*.

## Structure and economics

- **Legal form:** Limited Cooperative Association (LCA), US. Incorporated 2019.
- **Ownership:** Users of the Driver's Seat app could become cooperative members; members elected a majority of board seats, voted on major coop decisions, and were eligible for a share of profits.
- **Revenue model:** Sold mobility insights to city agencies to fund operations; "when the Driver's Seat Cooperative profits from insight sales, driver-owners receive dividends and share the wealth" (per Rockefeller Foundation grantee story).
- **Investment:** Per Ada Lovelace 2021 report, Driver's Seat was a 2019 graduate of the start.coop accelerator; PitchBook recorded a $300k angel investment in August 2020. Per the Rockefeller story, later investors included Impact America Fund, Draper Richards Kaplan, Mozilla, Ford Foundation, and Omidyar Network.

## Data model

- Drivers checked into the free app at the start of a shift; the app automatically logged time, miles, and pay by syncing (with driver permissions) into the gig apps they used (Lyft, Uber, Doordash, Spark, Shipt, Grubhub).
- Data types collected included: GPS and telemetry, workforce data (time/earnings/activities), mobility data (transportation mode, trip purpose).
- Insights aggregated across drivers and fed back as individual analytics on best times, locations, and apps for earnings optimisation.

## Scale (reported at peak)

Per the Rockefeller Foundation grantee story (grantee-impact piece, 2023):

- **5,000+ app users** as of December 2022, up from 2,000 in September 2021.
- **30+ US cities** covered, with expansion plans to New York City by end of 2023.
- **5 million trips tracked** since the 2019 founding.
- **13% average increase in income** reported by drivers using the app.

Partner organisations: Rideshare Drivers United, Los Deliveristas Unidos, Jobs with Justice.

## Counter-power mechanism

Driver's Seat combined two mechanisms:

1. **Individual-level tech workaround.** Drivers get decision-support analytics that counter the information asymmetry of the gig-app algorithms. Per founder Hays Witt: "On top of all these decisions, drivers are up against very powerful gig app algorithms that are trying to shape their decision-making in the companies' interests. At Driver's Seat, we're trying to make it easier for drivers to make choices that actually benefit them and boost their earnings by applying that same technology, that same sophisticated data science — but on behalf of workers."
2. **Collective [[data-cooperatives|data cooperative]].** Pooled driver data became a shared asset the coop could monetise (selling mobility insights to cities) and govern democratically.

## The sunset and its lessons

The Ada Lovelace 2021 case study flagged structural tensions even at the peak:

- **Drivers have both common and competing interests.** "In the ride-hailing market, in geographically limited areas, drivers may indeed have common interests, but they are also in competition with each other for rides."
- **Governance and data-rights documentation were thin** on the public website.
- **Sustainability strategy was unclear** beyond the mobility-insights sales line.

That the driversseat.co homepage is now a "Website Expired" Squarespace page, while the founder-team and core data-practice migrated to the **Workers' Algorithm Observatory at Princeton**, is consistent with the uptake/sustainability challenges the Ada Lovelace report identified as generic to [[data-cooperatives]] — not a proof of failure so much as a reminder that most live consumer data coops captured to date operate at sub-scale with fragile economics. *(editorial / synthesis)*

## Scope and limitations

- **Primary-source thinness.** The driversseat.co homepage no longer returns content; structural claims (founding year, LCA form, member governance) are triangulated from the Rockefeller Foundation grantee story, the Ada Lovelace 2021 case study, and earlier coverage.
- **Reported figures are self-reported.** The 13% income uplift and scale figures come from the Rockefeller grantee story and have not been independently audited in the captured sources.
- **Migration status not fully captured.** The transition to the Workers' Algorithm Observatory at Princeton was flagged in the WebSearch preamble but not captured as a primary source in this run.
