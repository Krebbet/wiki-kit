## Category mix — what's actually being traded

### Aggregate dominance (multi-source agreement)

- **Politics + Geopolitics dominate the largest contracts**, especially during election cycles and major geopolitical events (TRM `raw/.../01-trmlabs-21b-volume.md`; Sacra `raw/.../02-sacra-polymarket.md`; Grayscale `raw/.../07-grayscale-polymarket.md`).
- **Crypto remains a "consistently small share of activity at every user tier"** despite being one of the highest-count categories by market count (TRM, qualitative; 261 active Crypto markets confirmed by direct snapshot — see [[snapshots/polymarket-crypto-category-2026-05-13]]).
- **Sports and entertainment peak among mid-tier active traders and most-experienced market makers, not newcomers** (TRM).
- **Election-cycle concentration is real and large:** >70% of weekly Polymarket volume was election-related from June 2024 onward (Grayscale, Dune Analytics); by Sep 7 2024 only 5 of the top-10 markets by volume were non-election.

### Emerging-category specifics

- **Mention markets** (Polymarket-only — Kalshi doesn't currently match): 435 Pop Culture markets, 14 named subcategories ([[mention-markets]]; snapshot data 2026-05-12). Live examples in the May 2026 snapshot include three overlapping Elon Musk tweet-count markets summing **$12M combined** at 2026-05-13. See [[snapshots/polymarket-top-markets-2026-05-13]] (markets #7, #12, #16).
- **Crypto Pre-Market subcategory is the largest crypto sub-bucket** (115 of 261 markets at 2026-05-13). Token-launch FDV prediction markets (Opensea $100M-tranche 72% Yes; Predict.fun $50M 93% Yes) and token-sale commitment markets (Printr) are operationally distinct from the BTC/ETH directional flow.
- **Tariffs** (116 active markets, Feb 2026 per TRM) and **Ukraine ceasefire / China-Taiwan tensions** are macro-driven verticals with tens of thousands of users but per-market dollar volumes not separately disclosed in the captured corpus.

## User cohort structure

TRM's five-tier segmentation (Jan 1 – Mar 22 2026; maker-side addresses; trades ≥$5 collateral; top 500 markets by volume; multi-platform Polymarket + LIMITLESS + Opinion Market + predict.fun):

| Tier | Fill count | % of trades | Volume captured |
|---|---|---|---|
| First-time | 1 fill | <0.2% | $3.5M |
| Casual | 2–10 fills | not stated | not stated |
| Active (mid-frequency) | 11–1,000 fills | **44.7%** | $869M |
| Semi-pro | 1,000–10,000 fills | not stated | not stated |
| Market maker | >10,000 fills | **35.2%** | $774M |

Median trade size — **first-time: $30; market maker: $12**. Smaller median ticket on the most-active cohort is the spread-capture / market-making signature: large numbers of small fills around quoted spreads.

**Top-10 wallet P&L case studies (Jan 1 – Mar 22 2026, 80-day window):**

- Wallet #1 (top overall): **$6.2M** across Fed / World Cup / 2028 election — "spot mispriced probabilities across markets"; no single-vertical specialization.
- Wallet #2: **$3.35M** across 1M+ small trades in Oscars markets — algorithmic MM, median ~$12/trade.
- Wallets #3 + #4: **$3.26M combined** from selling *both* "hike" and "cut" Fed rate tokens ahead of a hold — macro-conviction "sell both tails" at tight directional uncertainty.
- Wallet #8: **$709K** in a single trading session — ETH price swings, timed entry and exit post-resolution.
- Wallet #9: **$664K** across NBA games — MM spread capture in a sports vertical.

Six of the top-10 traded *every day* over the 80-day window; two active 54 days; one active a single 30-event-driven days. Conclusion: the leaderboard is dominated by **continuously-active operators**, not opportunistic event traders — consistent with the log-linear profit-vs-transaction profile of arbitrageurs in [[arbitrage-taxonomy]] §7.4.

**Wallet-distribution shape (the question this page would most directly answer) is not captured.** Search-time pointers (per the ingest review) reference `theverge.com/news/922925/polymarkets-top-0-1-percent` reporting top-0.1% wallet shares; the wiki has not ingested that source. Open follow-up.

## Manipulation / integrity signals

Two specific patterns flagged in TRM:

- **Coordinated wallet clusters on event markets.** Four wallets turned ~$40K into **$872K** on Iran-strike markets (Jan–Feb 2026): shared bridge funding in a narrow time window, synchronized creation, prices $0.10–$0.80/share at entry, redeemed at $1.00. *Consistent with* insider information; not proven. Single event-cluster; small sample (4 wallets).
- **Wash-trading share.** Sacra cites a Columbia University study (Nov 2025) reporting **25% average / 60% peak (Dec 2024)** wash-share. This is in direct tension with [[polymarket-microstructure]]'s SF7 (Dubach 2026: median 1% / p99 10.6% / max 22.2%). Both findings are recorded; resolution proposal at [[conflicts/wash-trading-share]]. Methodology and time window differ; do not silently reconcile.

## Calibration — Polymarket's own claim

The Polymarket Predictions page (`raw/.../03-polymarket-predictions.md`) FAQ states:

- **Accuracy >90% one month before resolution.**
- **Accuracy >96% within four hours of resolution.**
- **Brier score 0.0843 across resolved markets.**

These are Polymarket-published numbers — self-report, not independent. Methodology not disclosed (which markets are included, how "accuracy" is binarized, whether all resolved markets or a subset). Cross-reference: superforecaster Brier 0.15–0.20 ([[llm-epistemic-calibration]]); aggregate prediction-market wisdom-of-crowds Brier 0.12–0.18 (Tetlock anchor). **A Brier of 0.0843 — if accurate — would imply Polymarket aggregate is materially better-calibrated than human superforecasters**, which is plausible (markets aggregate information; superforecasters are individuals) but unverified independently. Treat as a high-prior-credibility self-claim that warrants reproduction with a temporally-filtered eval using the methodology in [[llm-epistemic-calibration]].
