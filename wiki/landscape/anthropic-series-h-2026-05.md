# Anthropic Series H: $30B at $900B+ Valuation + First Profitable Quarter (May 2026)

Anthropic is closing a $30B Series H at a $900B+ pre-money valuation (Bloomberg, May 22, 2026; expected close week of June 2). If closed, this surpasses OpenAI's $852B March 2026 valuation and represents ~15x growth in ~14 months. Simultaneous investor disclosure (May 20, 2026) revealed Q2 2026 projected operating profit of $559M — Anthropic's first-ever profitable quarter — on $10.9B projected quarterly revenue. Anthropic cautioned profitability may not be sustained full-year due to planned compute expansion.

## Fundraising Mechanics

- **Round size:** $30B Series H; co-leads: Sequoia Capital, Dragoneer, Altimeter, Greenoaks (~$2B each, investor-disclosed)
- **Pre-money valuation:** $900B+ (Bloomberg, May 22, 2026 — "closing as soon as next week")
- **Prior round:** Series G, $30B at $380B valuation, led by Coatue + GIC, closed February 2026 — see [[google-anthropic-40b-2026-04]] for Google compute/equity backdrop
- **Valuation trajectory:** $60B (2024) → $380B (Feb 2026) → $900B+ (May 2026) — ~15x in ~14 months
- **Comparison:** Surpasses OpenAI's $852B valuation (March 2026) if closed at stated terms

## Q2 2026 Financials (Investor-Disclosed, May 20, 2026)

All figures below are Anthropic projections disclosed to investors; not audited.

| Metric | Q1 2026 | Q2 2026 (projected) |
|---|---|---|
| Quarterly revenue | $4.8B | $10.9B |
| Annualized revenue run-rate | ~$19B | ~$44B |
| Operating profit | (loss) | $559M |
| Compute cost per $1 revenue | $0.71 | $0.56 (target) |

- **Enterprise mix:** ~80% of revenue from enterprise customers (CNBC)
- **Claude Code:** $2.5B annualized revenue from the coding tool alone (TechFundingNews, sourcing CNBC)
- **Business subscriptions:** 4x growth since start of 2026
- **Benchmarking:** Revenue ramp described as faster than Google, Facebook, or Zoom at equivalent growth stage (Bloomberg)
- **Profitability caveat:** Anthropic explicitly told investors Q2 profit may not persist given planned compute spending increases — treat Q2 operating profit as a snapshot, not a run-rate

## Compute Efficiency Trajectory

- Q1 2026: 71¢ compute per $1 revenue — operationally marginal
- Q2 2026 target: 56¢ — meaningful efficiency gain in one quarter
- Driver: model distillation, inference optimization, scale amortization; Google TPU/compute deal (see [[google-anthropic-40b-2026-04]]) provides committed capacity at negotiated rates
- Risk: Anthropic has flagged aggressive compute capex ahead; the 56¢ ratio is a transitional data point, not a stable floor

## Enterprise Buyer Implications

- **Financial sustainability:** First profitable quarter removes near-term runway risk; enterprise buyers can now treat Anthropic as a going concern without requiring a near-term exit
- **Concentration risk persists:** Revenue heavily weighted toward a small number of enterprise accounts at this scale; 80% enterprise mix is a signal of depth, not breadth
- **Pricing floor:** Compute efficiency gains give Anthropic pricing room to compete with OpenAI on API cost without margin destruction — but planned capex expansion may suppress this for 2–4 quarters
- **Model continuity:** $30B infusion provides multi-year runway for model development independent of quarterly P&L — Claude 4/5 roadmap is not contingent on sustained profitability
- See [[llm-api-enterprise-share]] for market share context

## Talent Signal: Andrej Karpathy Hire (May 19, 2026)

- Karpathy (OpenAI co-founder, former Tesla AI director) joined Anthropic's pretraining team May 19, 2026 — one day before the investor financial disclosure
- Signal: Anthropic is investing in pretraining depth, not just inference/product; Claude model-quality trajectory is the competitive moat, not just API distribution
- Timing relative to Series H: adds credibility to valuation story for investors evaluating model-quality risk

| | Anthropic | OpenAI |
|---|---|---|
| Valuation (May 2026) | $900B+ (closing) | $852B (March 2026) |
| Q1 2026 revenue | $4.8B quarterly | ~$6.25B quarterly ($25B annualized) |

- OpenAI's confidential S-1 filing (May 22, 2026) and Anthropic's Series H closing in the same week frames a direct public narrative contest
- Anthropic's unit economics are materially better than OpenAI's despite lower absolute revenue — this is the "efficient growth" investor pitch
- OpenAI's $100B round (in progress per source) would further dilute valuation comparison; Anthropic's $900B+ is a snapshot

## Open Questions

- Will Q2 $559M operating profit prove durable, or is it a one-quarter artifact of compute timing?
- Does the $900B valuation assume Claude Code continuing at $2.5B+ annualized, or is that a separate upside scenario?
- Google's equity stake (from the $40B compute deal) creates governance complexity at $900B valuation — unclear if Google has pro-rata rights in Series H
- If OpenAI closes at $100B raised (implying higher valuation), does the Anthropic "surpasses OpenAI" narrative flip?
