# Wiki Index

*Wiki on collective consumer counter-power. **Mandate:** uncover strategies to combat consolidated industry power and monopoly pricing mechanisms, and determine technical solutions we could implement.*

**Two layers:**
- **Reference layer** — neutral, source-traceable documentation of extraction patterns and deployed counter-power mechanisms.
- **Strategy layer** (`strategies/`) — editorial / design-oriented content: strategic levers, development plans, implementation targets.

Catalog of all pages below. Updated on every ingest, research run, and strategy update.

| [[regulatory-responses]] | Consolidated counter-power landscape: DOJ, FTC, legislation (federal, NY A3008, municipal), EU/UK action, journalism, backlash, platform-cooperative exit-alternatives. Gap analysis and prioritised design-input candidates. |
| [[platform-cooperatives]] | Worker- and user-owned online platforms as an exit-alternative to platform capitalism. Scholz 2014 coinage; OECD 4-type typology; the capital-conundrum debate; federation-as-scaling pattern. |
| [[drivers-cooperative]] | NYC driver-owned rideshare cooperative (2021–). 15% commission vs Uber/Lyft 25–40%; focused on paratransit and NEMT. Concrete counter-power example against gig-economy dynamic pricing. |
| [[mondragon]] | Pre-digital foundational cooperative federation (Basque Country, 1956–). ~70K workers; 10 Basic Co-operative Principles; solidarity fund and wage-ratio cap. The institutional precursor platform-coops build from. |
| [[coopcycle]] | Federation of 72 bike-delivery worker cooperatives across 12 countries. OECD's featured case for the second-level cooperative (federation) pattern addressing the capital conundrum. |
| [[data-cooperatives]] | Anchor mechanism page. Member-owned data stewardship; Ada Lovelace 2021 legal-mechanisms framework + Mendonça et al. 2025 four-framework survey. Distinguishes coops from data trusts, commons, and unions. |
| [[collective-bargaining-for-data]] | Bargaining-lane mechanism page. CBI position paper (Vincent, Prewitt & Li 2025) + Porat 2024 experimental work on individual-level bargaining with pricing algorithms. |
| [[midata]] | Swiss nonprofit health-data cooperative, founded 2015. Open-source platform (Open MIDATA Server, GPLv3); OECD-cited canonical data-coop case. |
| [[drivers-seat-cooperative]] | US driver-owned data cooperative for gig workers (2019–). Reported 13% income uplift; has since sunset as a standalone org (driversseat.co homepage expired; activity migrated to Workers' Algorithm Observatory at Princeton). Distinct from [[drivers-cooperative]]. |
| [[open-tenant-screening]] | MIT researchers' crowdsourced audit of US tenant-screening services. Proto-cooperative transparency tool; the closest existing tenant-side counter to [[rental-housing-algorithmic-pricing|RealPage]]-class aggregation. |
| [[transparency-tools]] | Anchor mechanism page. Consumer-facing tools that make opaque market behaviour visible — price-history trackers, review-authenticity analysers, crowdsourced audit observatories. Includes Hannak et al. 2014 foundational detection methodology. |
| [[keepa]] | Commercial Amazon price-history browser extension. 4M Chrome users, 6B+ products, 11 locales. Keepa GmbH, Germany. Single-seller transparency tool exemplar. |
| [[paypal-honey]] | Auto-coupon browser extension; PayPal-acquired for ~$4B in 2020. Exposed from Dec 2024 for affiliate-link cookie-stuffing; lost ~8M Chrome users by end 2025. The canonical extractive-drift failure case for transparency tools. |
| [[fakespot]] | Review-authenticity analyser; Mozilla-acquired 2023, shut down July 2025. The canonical acquisition-and-sunset failure case; illustrates the sustainability challenges of grant-funded transparency tools. |
| [[markup-citizen-browser]] | The Markup's crowdsourced algorithmic-audit infrastructure (Oct 2020–). 1,000+ paid panelists, custom desktop app, Trail of Bits-audited redaction pipeline. Closest live template for a continuous pricing observatory. |
| [[consumer-collective-bargaining]] | Anchor mechanism page. Four-way taxonomy: GPOs (healthcare 600+/90% of hospital purchases), consumer cooperatives (Rochdale 1844 lineage), transactional group-buying (Tuángòu / Pinduoduo / Groupon), public-agency aggregation. |
| [[community-choice-aggregation]] | Public-agency US electricity aggregation. 9 authorising states; 15% of Americans across 1,850+ municipalities (2024). Opt-out default; California CCAs voluntarily procured 11 TWh above RPS mandate. Under-replicated template outside electricity. |
| [[park-slope-food-coop]] | Brooklyn consumer cooperative since 1973. ~17K members (July 2025). Member-labour model: 2h45m/6wks; 25% markup vs 26–100% at supermarkets. Live proof a Rochdale-lineage coop operates at urban grocery scale. |
| [[rei]] | 181-store US outdoor-recreation consumers' cooperative, founded 1938. One-vote-per-member; 2022 dividend-to-store-credit rebranding illustrates "cooperative drift" at scale. Counterpoint to Park Slope: depth vs breadth. |
| [[obfuscation]] | Mechanism anchor for counterfeit-data / decoy-behaviour counter-surveillance. Helen Nissenbaum theoretical anchor (2015 *Obfuscation*). Live tools: AdNauseam, TrackMeNot, Nightshade, Glaze. As of 2026-04-23: full strengths / weaknesses / technical-approach taxonomy / countermeasure landscape / legal-risk layer with direct-quote source attribution. |
| [[adversarial-data-poisoning]] | Mechanism anchor for technical approaches to coordinated data manipulation. Wang et al. 2024 three-dimension taxonomy of recommender-system poisoning attacks (Component-Specific, Goal-Driven, Capability-Probing). Solanki et al. 2025 Algorithmic Collective Action formalism under differential privacy — first formal lower bounds on collective success; surprise reversal that firms can use DP to blunt consumer ACA. |
| [[browser-fingerprinting]] | Mechanism anchor for the watcher-side technique stack and detection arms race. Lawall 2024 catalogue (Canvas / WebGL / Audio / Font / Screen / WebRTC / CSS + entropy figures). Venugopalan et al. 2024 FP-Inconsistent: naive per-attribute rotation defeated at ~97% TNR via cross-attribute inconsistency detection. Documents the "blending in" paradox. |
| [[pricing-algorithm-taxonomy]] | Mechanism anchor for seller-side pricing-algorithm families and the data each one consumes (added 2026-04-23). Six families: GLM/contextual bandit (Zhao et al. 2024), neural demand estimation (Safonov 2024), retail-offer bandit (Tanković & Šajina 2025), airline stochastic DP (Williams 2018), RealPage hub-and-spoke (Yale TAP 2025), Uber per-trip opaque (FAccT 2025). Plus six-category data-input catalogue. Critical finding: airline RM and neural retail demand use **no individual identity features** — identity-based disruption is structurally ineffective against those families. |
| [[adnauseam]] | Ad-click obfuscation browser extension (2014–; Howe/Nissenbaum/Zer-Aviv). Banned from Chrome Web Store Jan 2017. MIT Tech Review 2021 test: $100 earned on test AdSense account. Canonical platform-ban precedent for obfuscation tools. |
| [[nightshade-glaze]] | UChicago SAND Lab (Ben Zhao) image-poisoning tools. Glaze (defensive, 6M+ downloads) + Nightshade (offensive, collective). Working case of cost-asymmetry levered adversarial data-poisoning against AI scraping. |
| [[privacy-badger]] | EFF algorithmic tracker-learning browser extension. Free/OSS across Chrome/Firefox/Edge/Brave/Opera. Template for learn-the-pattern-then-block variants of pricing-detection tools. |
| [[boycott-apps]] | Buycott (UPC-barcode boycott campaigns; 600M+ barcode database) + Goods Unite Us (7K companies / 2M users / FEC political-alignment data). Live implementations of collective-redirection lever. |
| [[open-food-network]] | Open-source farmer marketplace platform. 1% of sales fee; multi-country federation (UK, Canada, Australia). CoopCycle-style federation pattern applied to food supply. Direct-payment flow. |
| [[time-banks-lets]] | 1 hour = 1 Time Dollar reciprocal service exchange. TimeBanks.org + hOurworld international network. $300–1,200/yr member savings. True disintermediation — transactions bypass the monetary pricing layer. |
| [[noyb]] | Austrian non-profit (2017–); Max Schrems; 4,400+ members. Strategic-litigation model — Schrems II invalidated Privacy Shield; forced €50M Google fine and €5M Spotify fine. Template for consumer-union-with-war-chest lever. |
| [[algorithmwatch]] | Berlin/Zurich ADM-accountability nonprofit (2016–). Research + fellowships + policy advocacy on automated decision-making. Template for pricing-algorithm watchdog organisation. |
| [[auto-enrollment-opt-out]] | US state auto-IRA programmes (OregonSaves 2017–, CalSavers 2019–). 30-day opt-out, 5% default contribution. Second working US precedent (alongside CCAs) for opt-out default aggregation outside electricity. |
